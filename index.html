We are 95% confident that the true slope for \(\beta_1\) is between 0.0129 and 0.0149. These values represent that for each additional offensive yard, there is (predicted) an additional \(x\) touchdowns, \(x\) being between 0.0129 and 0.0149 with 95% confidence.

(iii)

Claim: A team can add one more touchdown to their score-board by improving 75 more offensive yards.

\(H_0: \beta_1 = \frac{1}{75} \approx .0133\)

\(H_\alpha: \beta_1 \neq \frac{1}{75} \approx .0133\)

B1_hat <- coefficients(summary(m1))["Off.Yards","Estimate"]
B1_se <- coefficients(summary(m1))["Off.Yards", "Std. Error"]
B1_null <- 0.0133
t_stat <- (B1_hat - B1_null) / B1_se

#Our t statistic
t_stat
## [1] 1.24574
#Our p value
2*pt(q = t_stat, df = nrow(cfb) - 2, lower.tail=F)
## [1] 0.2151353

With a t statistic of 1.246 and 128 degrees of freedom, we obtain a p-value of .215. That is, there is a 21.5% chance we would have achieved a sample slope estimate of 0.0139 given that the null hypothesis is true. Therefore, we do not have significant evidence to reject the null hypothesis, that is, that a team can add one more touchdown to their score-board by improving 75 more offensive yards.
